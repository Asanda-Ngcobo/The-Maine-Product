Solve for x by factoring

Factoring is the easiest form and should always be your first option. If you are not sure how to do factoring, click the link below to take you to our maths basics page.

×
  1. 9x2 - 12x -5
  2. First, multiply A by C. 9 x -5 = -45
  3. Then, find the factors of the answer you got from your first step. 45 x -1, -45 x 1, 9 x -5, -9 x 5, 15 x -3 and -15 x 3.
  4. Replace B with factors from the second step that gives you B when adding them together. -15 + 3 = -12, therefore 9x2 - 15x + 3x -5
  5. Grouping: surround the first two terms with their own parenthesis (), and do the same for the last two terms. (9x2 - 15x), (+ 3x -5)
  6. Factorize both terms. When factorizing both terms, you probably going to end up with the same terms inside parenthesis. 3x(3x -5), +1(+3x -5)
  7. Because terms inside parenthesis are the same, take one of them and solve for x:
    3x - 5 = 0
    3x = 5
    x = 5/3.
    Then take the ones outside of the parenthesis, group them together, and solve for x:
    3x + 1
    3x = -1
    x = -1/3.

Solve for x by completing the square

Completing the square should be only used if the quadratic equation cannot be factorized but a = 1 and b is an even number. If you do not sure what is an even number you can click the link below to take you to our maths basics page. ax2+ bx + c

×
  1. x2 - 10x -18 = 0
  2. First, add 18 to both sides. x2 - 10x -18 + 18 = 0 + 18, which gives you x2 - 10x = 18.
  3. Then, B = (b/2)2, (-10/2)2 = 25.
  4. Add this 25 on both sides of the equation, x2 - 10x + 25 = 18 + 25
  5. x2 - 10x + 25 = 45
  6. x2 - 10x + 25 = (x + b/2)2
    = (x + (-10/2))2
    = (x - 5)2
  7. Therefore, (x - 5)2 = 43
  8. √(x - 5)2 = √43
  9. x - 5= √43
  10. x = 5 ±√43
