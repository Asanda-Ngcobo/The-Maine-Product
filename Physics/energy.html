Introduction to kinetic, potential and mechanical energy

Mechanical energy (kinetic energy or potential energy) is the energy of either an object in motion or the energy that is stored in objects by their position.

Elastic potential energy

Elastic potential energy is the potential energy stored by stretching or compressing an elastic object by an external force such as the stretching of a spring.

Law of conservation of energy or First Law of thermodynamics

The law of conservation of energy states that energy can neither be created nor destroyed - only converted from one form of energy to another.

What is energy, work and power

×
  1. Energy is the ability to do work.
  2. Work is the force applied over certain displacement (W = F.d). However, we prefer including cos θ in your equation because it is very useful, especially when calculating work done on inclined planes. Therefore, we prefer equation W = F.d.cosθ. Work is measured in Joules, meaning Joules is the same as Newtons (a unit of force) x Meters (a unit of displacement)
  3. Power is the work done over a period of time (P = F.d.cosθ/t). Power is measured in Watts, meaning Watts is the same as (Newtons x meters/seconds). The more work done over less period of time, the more power.

Work-Energy Theorem: (Net Work = final Kinetic Energy - initial Kinetic Energy)

The work-energy theorem states that the net work done by the forces on an object equals the change in its kinetic energy.
