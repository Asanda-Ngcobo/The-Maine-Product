Electric Field

An electric field (sometimes E-field) is the physical field that surrounds electrically charged particles and exerts a force on all other charged particles in the field, either attracting or repelling them.
The electric field is radially outward from a positive charge and radially in toward a negative point charge.


Electric field strength at a given point

E = k.Q/r2, where Q = is the charge creating the electric field of interest, k = is Coulomb's constant, and r2 or d2 is the distance of a given point away from Q. e.g. Calculate the electric field strength of charge 1, 2 meters away if charge 1 has a charge of 1 x 10-6
E = 9 x 109.1 x 10-6/22
= 2250 N/C


Electric field experienced by q, when given a force exerted by Q to q

E = F/q. e.g. Charge 1 exerts a force of 2250 N to charge 2 of 1C
E = 2250 N / 1C
E = 2250 N/C

Calculating the force

F = k.Q.q/r2, where F = is the force created by Q, and q = is the charge experiencing the field of interest. use this equation when not given the force.

E.g. using the info given above, F = 9 x 109.1 x 10-6/22. 1C/ 22 = 2250N
Therefore Q exerts a force of 2250N and also experiences a force of 2250N from q (Newton's Third law)

F = E.q, use this equation when given electric field strength. E.g. using the info above, if q experiences an electric force of 2250N/C from Q, what is the magnitude of force q experience from Q
F = 2250 N/C . 1C = 2250N

Electric potential energy

The energy a charge in an electric field possesses, which gives it the ability to do work and it depends on three things:


  • Type of charge - positive or negative
  • Amount of charge
  • Strength of the electric field

Gravitational potential energy is equal to gravitational force x-height, remember height (h) and distance (d) are similar, the difference refers to up-down or down-up (h), whereas distance is left-right or right-left (d).
Therefore, PEelectric = electric force x distance
Hence, PEelectric = k.Q.q/r2x r, the r at the top will cancel one of the r squares, so the final equation is PEelectric = k.Q.q/r

Another equation for PEelectric is PEelectric = q.E.r because electric force is also equal to q.E

Electric potential

The electric potential energy per unit charge (1C)


V = PEelectric divided by unit charge(q) = k.Q.q/r.q, the small q's will cancel each other, leaving the final equation as V = k.Q/r, measured in volts.

Another equation for V is q.E.r/q, the small q's will cancel each other, leaving the final equation as V = E.r

Electric potential vs voltage

Voltage is the electric potential difference between two charged points


E.g.

  • Voltage from point a to point b is ΔV = Vb - Va
    = PEb/q - PEa/q
    = 1/q(PEb - PEa)
    = 1/qΔPE

    • Positively charged particles (e.g. protons) move from high electric potential to low electric potential.
    Negatively charged particles (e.g. electrons) moves from low electric potential to high electric potential.

    Workdone (W) to move the charge from point a to b when given voltage or electrical potential for both point a and b.

  • Remember based on work-energy theorem, W = ΔKinetic energy (ΔKE) and based on mechanical energy, ΔKE = -ΔPotential energy (-ΔPE)
  • Therefore, -ΔPE = W or -W = ΔPE
  • Thus, ΔV can be represented as ΔV = 1/q(-W)
  • You can then solve for work by multiplying both sides with -q, -q x ΔV = 1/q(-W) x -q
    -qΔV = 1x -W/-1
    -qΔV = W
