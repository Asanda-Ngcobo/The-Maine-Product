One dimension-free fall problem, finding the final velocity 1

  1. In Newtonian physics, free fall is any motion of a body where gravity is the only force acting upon it.
  2. Gravity is Represented as 9.8 m/s2 towards the surface of the earth.
    Therefore, When an object is in free fall, moving towards the surface of the earth, gravity accelerates the object by 9.8 m/s every second.
    The table below shows what will happen in 4 seconds, to an object when dropped from a position above the earth's surface.
    t(s) 1s 2s 3s 4s
    V(m/s) 9.8 m/s 19.6 m/s 29.4 m/s 39.2 m/s
    As shown in the table, gravity will increase the object's velocity by 9.8 m/s every second until reaching the earth's surface, because the object is moving at the same direction as the gravity.
    Hence, when the object is moving down(towards the earth's surface), it is recommended gravity be positive (9,8 m/s), velocity be positive, and displacement to be negative (initial displacement is greater than zero (Yi) and final displacement is equal to zero (Yf), Yf - Yi < 0).
  3. In contrast, When an object is in free fall, moving away from the surface of the earth, gravity decelerates the object by 9.8 m/s every second.
    The table below shows what will happen in 4 seconds, to an object up, away from the earth's surface at 39.4 m/s.
    t(s) 1s 2s 3s 4s
    V(m/s) 39.2 m/s 29.4 m/s 19.6 m/s 9.8 m/s
    The table above demonstrates that gravity will decrease the object's velocity by 9.8 m/s every second until reaching 0 m/s and then accelerating it back to Earth again.
    Hence, when the object is moving up(away from the earth's surface), it is recommended gravity be positive (-9,8 m/s), velocity be negative, and displacement be position (initial displacement is usually less than final displacement (Yf), Yf - Yi > 0).

    4. Three equations of motion in projectiles

    • Vf = Vi + gt
    • Y = Vit + 1/2gt2
    • V2f = V2i + 2g(Yf - Yi)
  5. Displacement, distance, or height is represented as Y because projectiles deals will up and down motion, which is y-axis motion, and a is replaced by g because g is what accelerates or decelerates free falls.

Introduction to two dimension free fall

×

  1. 2 dimension free-falls or projectiles

    2 dimension free-falls involve the movement of an object in both the y-axis and the x-axis.
    Since in free falls, gravity is the only force acting on an object, y-acceleration or deceleration is equal to ± 9.8 m/s.
    However, x-acceleration or deceleration is 0 m/s.
When working with 2D free fall problems, basic knowledge of Trigonometry and Geometry is very important, particularly SOH-CAH-TOA.
