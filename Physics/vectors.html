The difference between Speed, velocity and acceleration

    • Acceleration

      It is the change in velocity at a given time (Vf - Vi/t).
      Vi, being the velocity measured at the start, Vf being velocity measured at the end, and time being the seconds it took for the moving object to be moving from Vi to Vf.
      Units of velocity is meters per second (m/s) and units of time is seconds (s).
      Therefore units for acceleration is m/s2
    • E.g, a car from a stationary position, starts moving at 2 m/s2, at what Velocity would it be moving in 6 seconds.
      t(s) 1s 2s 3s 4s 5s 6s
      V(m/s) 2 m/s 4 m/s 6 m/s 8 m/s 10 m/s 12 m/s
      Therefore, the car will be moving at 12 ms in 6 seconds.
      Calculation proof, a = Vf - Vi/t
      a + Vi = (Vf - Vi/t) + Vi (since we are solving for Vf, Vi is algebraically moved to the other side by adding Vi on both sides of the equation.)
      t * a + Vi = Vf/t * t (Now, we move t to the other side by multiplying by it on both sides.)
      at + Vi = Vf (resulting in the first equation of motion)
    • Now we can find the final velocity mathematically.
      Since, the car was stationary at he start, Vi is therefore equal to 0
      Vf = at + Vi
      Vf = 2 m/s2 * 6 s + 0 m/s
      Vf = 12 m/s2 * s ( s will cancel one of m/s2, s, leaving us with m/s as the final unit)
      Therefore, Vf = 12 m/s
    • If you haven't noticed, Vf = at + Vi is the same as y = mx + C, where Vf is y, a is m, t is x, and Vi is C
    • Tip: Knowledge and understanding of algebra is very important when working with equations of motion.

  1. 2nd equation (Finding the displacement)

    X = Vit + 1/2(at2)
    Vit = (m/s) * s = m (because Ss cancel each other)
    1/2(at2) = (m/s2) * s2 = m (because S2s cancel each other)

  2. at = change in velociy

    at = (Vf - Vi/t) * t
    at = Vf - Vi (Because, the t's cancel each other).
    Therefore, at = ΔV

  3. 1/2at is equal to average velocity (X/t)

    Change in velocity divided by 2 is the average velocity.
    Therefore, 1/2at is equal to average velocity (m/s) * time (s).

    4. All four equations of motion

    • Vf = Vi + at
    • X = Vit + 1/2at2
    • V2f = V2i + 2ax
    • V2f = V2i + 2a(xf - xi)
