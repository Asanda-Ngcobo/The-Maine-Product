  1. Applied Force

    is a force that is applied to an object by a person or another object. If a person is pushing a desk across the room, then there is an applied force acting upon the object.
    Represented as Fa

  2. Gravitational Force

    Gravity is the force by which a planet or other body draws objects toward its center. Earth's gravitational force is 9.807 m/s2.
    Represented as Fg or G

  3. Normal Force

    The normal force is the force that surfaces exert to prevent solid objects from passing through each other. The normal force is a contact force. If two surfaces are not in contact, they can't exert a normal force on each other. Represented as Fn
    Note: Normal force is always perpendicular to the surface the object is placed on.

  4. Frictional Force

    Friction is the force that opposes the motion of a solid object sliding or rolling over another.
    Represented as f

  5. Tension Force

    the force transmitted through a rope, string, or wire when pulled by forces acting from opposite sides. The tension force is directed over the length of the wire and pulls energy equally on the bodies at the ends.
    Represented as Ft

Working with resultant or net force

×

    Resultant or Net force

  1. Forces in the same direction and parallel to one another.

    When forces acting on an object, are in the same direction and parallel to one another, the resultant force is the sum (∑) of the magnitude of the forces.
    E.g. FR = F1 + F2

  2. Forces in opposite directions and parallel to one another.

    When forces acting on an object, are in the same direction and parallel to one another, the resultant force is the difference of the magnitude of the forces.
    E.g. FR = F1 - F2

  3. Forces perpendicular to one another.

    When Forces acting on an object are perpendicular to each other they form a right-angle, making the horizontal force, x-axis force, left-right or right-left force, or East-west or West-East force magnitude is the Adjacent, and the vertical force, y-axis force, up-down or down-down force, or North-South or South-North force magnitude is the Opposite. Therefore the resultant force is the Hypotenuse.
    Therefore The resultant force can be found by using Pythagorean theorem:
    (Hypotenuse)2 = (Adjacent)2 + (Opposite)2
    Hypotenuse = √ (Adjacent)2 + (Opposite)2
    Hence, Resultant Force (FR) = √(Fx)2 + (Fy)2
    E.g. a 50 N East force and a 120 N South acting on an object, what is the resultant force?
    (FR) = √(Fx)2 + (Fy)2
    (FR) = √502 + 1202
    (FR) = 130 N
    Finding the angle of the resultant force
    The reference angle is θ = tan-1(Fy / fx)
    If, Forces perpendicular to each other are North and West, the resultant angle is 0 + reference angle
    If, Forces perpendicular to each other are North and East, the resultant angle is 180 - reference angle
    If, Forces perpendicular to each other are South and East, the resultant angle is 180 + reference angle
    If Forces perpendicular to each other are South and West, the resultant angle is 360 - reference angle
    What is the angle of the above resultant force?
    reference angle is θ = tan-1(Fy / fx)
    reference angle is θ = tan-1(120 N / 50 N)
    reference angle is θ = 67.4° Resultant angle is therefore = 180° + reference angle
    Resultant angle is therefore = 180° + 67.4°
    Resultant angle = 247.4°

  4. Forces on angle

    Things to keep in mind with forces on the angle
    Fx = F Cosθ
    Fy = F Sinθ
    FR = √(Fx)2 + (Fy)2
    reference angle is θ = tan-1(Fy / fx)
    E.g. a 100 N West and 150 N North-West are acting on an object, what is the resultant force and angle?
    Fwestx
    Fwestx = 100 Cosθ
    Fwestx = 100 Cos0
    Fwestx = 100 N

    Fwesty
    Fwesty = 100 Sinθ
    Fwesty = 100 Sin0
    Fwesty = 0 N

    Fnorth-westx
    Fnorth-westx = 150 Cosθ
    Fnorth-westx = 150 Cos30
    Fnorth-westx = 129.9 N

    Fnorth-westy
    Fnorth-westy = 150 Sinθ
    Fnorth-westy = 150 Sin30
    Fnorth-westy = 75 N

    Sum of Fx
    Sum of Fx = Fwestx + Fnorth-westx
    Sum of Fx = 100 N + 129.9 N
    Sum of Fx = 229.9 N

    Sum of Fy
    Sum of Fy = Fwesty + Fnorth-westy
    Sum of Fy = 0 N + 75 N
    Sum of Fy = 75 N

    Resultant Force
    FR = √(Fx)2 + (Fy)2
    FR = √229.9 N + 75 N
    FR = 241.8 N

    Resultant force angle:
    reference angle is θ = tan-1(Fy / fx)
    reference angle is θ = tan-1(75 N / 229.9 N)
    reference angle is θ = 18.1°

    Resultant force angle = 0 + reference angle
    Resultant force angle = 0 + 18.1°
    Resultant force angle = 18.1°

  1. Working with forces acting on an object

    First thing first, label all the forces acting on an object before doing anything.

  2. Object on a frictionless flat surface pushed or pulled at no angle

    Forces acting on it are Gravitational force, Normal force, and Applied force by the push or pull.
    Gravitational = Normal force, therefore will cancel each other, leaving the applied force as the net force.

  3. Object on a friction flat surface pushed or pulled at no angle

    Forces acting on it are Gravitational force, Normal force, Frictional force, and Applied force by the push or pull.
    Gravitational = Normal force, therefore will cancel each other, leaving the difference between applied force and frictional force as the net force (Net Force = Applied force - Frictional force).

  4. Object on a friction-flat surface pushed at an angle

    Forces acting on it are Gravitational force, Normal force, Frictional force, and Applied force by the push.
    Normal force = Gravitational force (mg) + y component of the applied force(Applied forceSinθ)
    Net force = x component of the applied force (Applied forceCosθ) - Frictional force.
    A 50 kg on a flat surface with a 12 N frictional force is pushed at a 25° angle by an 80 N force. What is the Normal force magnitude?
    Normal force = 50 kg x 9.8 m/s2 + 80NSin25
    Normal force = 490N + 33.81N
    Normal force = 523.81N

    The Normal force is always equal to forces acting perpendicular and downward to the surface, therefore when an object is pushed at an angle, the surface will experience force of gravity plus the y-component force of the push applied force because when the object is pushed the y-component force is acting perpendicular to the surface downward.

    Calculate the net force. Net force = 80NCos25 - 12N
    Net force = 72.50 - 12N
    Net force = 60.5 N

  5. Object on a friction flat surface pulled at an angle

    Forces acting on it are Gravitational force, Normal force, Frictional force, and Applied force by the pull.
    Normal force = Gravitational force (mg) - y component of the applied force(Applied forceSinθ)
    Net force = x component of the applied force (Applied forceCosθ) - Frictional force.
    A 50 kg on a flat surface with a 12 N frictional force is pulled at a 25° angle by an 80 N force. What is the Normal force magnitude?
    Normal force = 50 kg x 9.8 m/s2 - 80NSin25
    Normal force = 490N - 33.81N
    Normal force = 456.19

    The Normal force is always equal to forces acting perpendicular and downward to the surface, therefore when an object is pulled at an angle, the surface will experience force of gravity minus the y-component force of the pull applied force, because when the object is pulled at an angle the y-component force of the applied force will act upward, against gravity, making the surface experience a force of gravity minus y-component of the applied force.

    Calculate the net force. Net force = 80NCos25 - 12 N
    Net force = 72.50 N - 12 N
    Net force = 60.5 N

  6. Object on a frictionless inclined surface pulled or pushed up the surface

    Forces acting on it are Gravitational force, Normal force, and Applied force by the pull or push.
    Normal force = x component of the gravitational force(mg *Cosθ)
    Net force = Applied force - y component of gravity (mg * Sinθ).
    A 50 kg on an inclined surface of 25° is pulled up the slope by an 80 N force. What is the Normal force magnitude?
    Normal force = 50 kg x 9.8 m/s2 * Cos25
    Normal force = 490 * Cos25
    Normal force = 444.09 N

    Calculate the net force. Net force = 80N - 490N * Sin25
    Net force = 80N - 207.08N
    Net force = -127.08N
    Since the applied force is acting against the y-component force of gravity, the net force = Applied force - y component of the gravitational force (mg * Sinθ)

  7. Object on a frictionless inclined surface pulled or pushed down the surface

    Forces acting on it are Gravitational force, Normal force, and Applied force by the pull or push.
    Normal force = x component of the gravitational force(mg *Cosθ)
    Net force = Applied force + y component of the gravitational force (mg * Sinθ).
    A 50 kg on an inclined surface of 25° is pushed down the slope by an 80 N force. What is the Normal force magnitude?
    Normal force = 50 kg x 9.8 m/s2 * Cos25
    Normal force = 490 * Cos25
    Normal force = 444.09 N

    Calculate the net force. Net force = 80N + 490N * Sin25
    Net force = 80N + 207.08N
    Net force = 287.08N
    Since the applied force is acting in the same direction as the y-component force of gravity, the net force = Applied force + y component of the gravitational force (mg * Sinθ)
