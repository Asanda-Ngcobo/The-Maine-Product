Frequency is the number of oscillations per second (F = 1/T).
1 representing 1 oscillation and T representing 1 second, since oscillations have no units, Frequency units is therefore, s-1.
However, Frequency is measured in Hz, telling us that 1 s-1 is equal to 1 Hz.
This makes more sense when calculating wave speed which is V = F.λ. Wavelength is measured in meters and Frequency in Hz, therefore wave speed units is m.Hz, but that doesn't make sense because we all know units of speed is m.s-1 not m.Hz.
But if you understand that 1 Hz is equal to 1 s-1, then you can easily understand that m.Hz is equivelant m.s-1.
