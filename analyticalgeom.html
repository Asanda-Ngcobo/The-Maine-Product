Understand Triangles

×

  1. Triangles

    2. A triangle is a three-sided polygon. The three sides are connected with each other end to end at a point, which forms the angles of the triangle. The sum of all three angles of the triangle is equal to 180 degrees. Understanding triangles is very important in both Geometry and Trigonometry.

  2. Classification of triangles

    Triangles can be classified by either their angles or sides.

  3. Classification with angles

    Acute triangle, a triangle with all angle less than 90°.
    Right triangle, a triangle with 2 angles less than 90° and one angle equal to 90°.
    Obtuse triangle, a triangle with 2 angles less than 90° and one angle greater 90°.

  4. Classification with sides

    Equilateral triangle is a triangle with all three sides equal to one another.
    Isosceles triangle is a triangle with only two of the three sides equal to each another.
    Scalene triangle is a triangle with none of three sides equal to one another.

  5. Additionally,


    Acute triangle can be Scalene, Isosceles, or Equilateral. However, obtuse and right triangle can either be Scalene or Isosceles, never Equilateral.

  6. Last but definitely not least

    The sum of any given triangle angles always add up to 180°.

What is slope or gradient?

×

  1. Calculatig the slope or gradient and the distance between two points of a straight line.

  2. A line can be defined as a connection of two points
  3. Slope or gradient which can usually represented by small letter (m) is visualize or describes the steepness of the line and can be calculated as follows:
    m = ΔY-axis ÷ ΔX-axis
    ΔY-axis = y-coordinate of the final point of the line - y-coordinate of the starting point of the line.

    ΔX-axis = x-coordinate of the final point of the line - x-coordinate of the starting point of the line.

    In Mathematics, points are usually represented alphabetically, e.g. point A (-2, 0) and point B (4, 3),
    Since alphabetically A comes before B, A is therefore the starting point and B is the final point.

    Hence, ΔY-axis = 3 - 0
    ΔY-axis = 3
    and
    ΔX-axis = 4 - (-2)
    ΔX-axis = 4 + 2
    ΔX-axis = 6

    Now we can find the gradient:
    m = ΔY-axis ÷ ΔX-axis
    m = 3 ÷ 6
    m = 0.5
