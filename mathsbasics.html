Number Classification

×
    Classification of numbers is the core of mathematics since mathematics is mainly based on numbers. Hence, one working or studying mathematics should be familiar with classification of Numbers.

  1. Natural numbers

    2. All numbers starting from 1 upwards. e.g., 1, 2, 3, 4, .....

  2. Whole numbers

    Includes all Natural numbers, plus 0. e.g., 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, .....

  3. Integers

    All whole numbers, plus negative numbers. e.g., ..., -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, ...

  4. Rational numbers

    All intergers, plus fractions and decimals. However, fractions must be made up of intergers and the denominator should not be zero (0).

  5. Irrational numbers


    Numbers that cannot be represented as simple fractions. e.g., √2, √5, π = 3.14..., e = 2. 718281.., Phi = 1,618033.., i = √-1, etc. All rational numbers are not Irrational numbers.

  6. Real numbers

    All Rational and Irrational Numbers.

Odd vs Even numbers

×

  1. Even Numbers

    Even numbers are numbers that give you an Integer (All numbers including negative and positive numbers, and zero (0), no fractions. E.g., ...-3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3...), when divided by 2. for example, ...-6, -4, -2, 0, 2, 4, 6, ...

  2. Odd numbers

    Numbers that do not give you an integer when divided by 2. E.g., 1, 3, 5, 6, ...

  3. Adding Even and Odd Numbers

    EVEN + EVEN = EVEN
    E.g., 4 + 4 = 8
    6 + 6 = 12
    EVEN + ODD = ODD
    3 + 4 = 7
    17 + 24 = 41
    ODD + ODD = EVEN
    3 + 3 = 6
    13 + 13 = 26
  4. Multiplying Even and Odd numbers
    5. EVEN x EVEN = EVEN
    2 x 2 = 4
    12 x 12 = 144
    ODD x EVEN = EVEN
    3 x 4 = 12
    9 X 10 = 90
    ODD x ODD = ODD
    3 x 3 = 9
    15 x 15 = 225

Multiplying and dividing and integers

×

  1. Multiplying intergers with the same sign

    The Product (The number you get when you multiply numbers together.), should have a positive sign. e.g.,
    -3 x -3 = 9
    3 x 3 = 9

  2. Multiplying intergers with different signs

    The product should is always negative. E.g.,
    -3 x 3 = -9
    -5 x 5 = -25

  3. Diving intergers with the same sign

    The Quotient (The number you get when you divide numbers together.), is always positive, just like when multiplying. E.g.,
    -3 ÷ -3 = 1
    10 ÷ 2 = 5

  4. Diving intergers with different signs

    The quotient is always negative, just like when multiplying. E.g.,
    -3 ÷ 3 = -1
    10 ÷ -2 = -5

Adding and substracting integers

×

  1. Adding Intergers

  2. If the signs are the same

    Add the absolute (A number that is neither negative nor positive), values, and the sum should therefore have the sign of both values.
    5 + 5 = ?
    Absolute values are 5 and 5, the Sum (The number you get when you add numbers together.) of that is 10 and they both positive(+)
    Therefore, the final answer is 10.
    -3 + (-7) = ?
    absolute values are 3 and 7, the sum of that is 10 and they are both negative(-)
    Therefore, the final answer is -10.

  3. If the signs are different

    Subtract the smaller absolute value from the greater absolute value and the difference should carry the sign of the greater value.
    5 + (-3) = ?
    absolute values are 5 and 3, 3 is smaller than 5, therefore subtract 3 from 5 ( 5 - 3 = 2).
    Since 5 is positive, the difference (The number you get when you subtract numbers together.), is therefore also positive (+2).

    3 + (-5) = ?
    absolute values are 5 and 3, 3 is smaller than 5, therefore subtract 3 from 5 ( 5 - 3 = 2).
    Since 5 is negative, the difference is also negative (-2).

  4. Substracting Intergers

  5. When subtracting intergers with different signs, having the negative integer on the right.

    Add the absolute values and the sum should be positive e.g.:
    5 - (-3) = ?
    absolute values are 5 and 3, the sum of that is 8 and the value on the left is positive(+)
    Therefore, the final answer is +8.

  6. Substracting intergers with different signs, having the negative integer on the left.

    7. Add the absolute values and the sum should be negative e.g.:
    -3 - (+5) = ?
    Simply, add absolute values (5 and 3), the sum of that is 8 and the value on the left is negative(-)
    Therefore, the final answer is -8.

  7. When subtracting intergers with the same signs, having the greater integer on the left.

    Subtract the smaller absolute value from the greater absolute value and the difference should be negative.
    -5 - (-3) = ?
    absolute values are 5 and 3, 3 is smaller than 5, therefore subtract 3 from 5 ( 5 - 3 = 2).
    S, the difference is therefore negative (-2).

  8. When subtracting intergers with the same signs, having the greater integer on the right.

    Subtract the smaller absolute value from the greater absolute value and the difference should be positive.
    -3 - (-5) = ?
    absolute values are 5 and 3, 3 is smaller than 5, therefore subtract 3 from 5 ( 5 - 3 = 2).
    The difference is therefore also positive (+2).
